# IdeaverseLIFE Vault Guidelines for Claude

## Core Philosophy
This vault follows the "Ideaverse" methodology. The primary goal is to maintain a sacred space for original thinking and future-proof plain text notes. 
- **Keep it simple**: Avoid creating too many folders. Keep notes in broad categories until patterns naturally emerge.
- **Bet on text**: Use standard Markdown formatting to ensure files remain lightweight and readable across decades.

## Folder Structure (ACE + X Framework)
When categorizing or creating notes, strictly follow this framework:
- **Atlas**: Put timeless ideas, concepts, and knowledge notes here.
- **Calendar**: Put time-based notes (daily notes, journals, meeting logs) here.
- **Efforts**: Put time-bound notes (active projects, tasks, ongoing work) here.
- **Extras (X)**: Put any other files or attachments that do not fit the above categories here.

## AI Interaction Rules
1. **Respect Original Thought**: Never overwrite or delete user-generated notes without explicit confirmation.
2. **Separate AI Content**: Clearly distinguish AI-generated summaries, rewrites, or brainstorms from human original thoughts. If you generate new content, append a tag like `#AI-generated` or place it in a designated AI subfolder.
3. **Link, Don't Duplicate**: When appropriate, suggest double-bracket links `[[like this]]` to connect related concepts instead of copying the text.

## Common Operations & Skills
When asked to perform common tasks, follow these workflows:
- **Cleanup / Categorization**: If you find loose notes in the root directory, analyze their content and move them into the appropriate ACE (Atlas, Calendar, Efforts) folders.
- **Daily/Weekly Digest**: When asked to summarize recent activities, extract notes from the `Calendar` folder based on the date, compile them into a simple Markdown list, and highlight key action items.
